About American Law

Project Overview

American Law is a comprehensive collection of legal documents, cases, and statutes from across the United States. Our mission is to make legal information more accessible and usable for researchers, legal professionals, and the public.

While projects such as the Free Law Project focus on Federal and State laws, we have instead focused on municipal and county-level legal documents. This includes city ordinances, local regulations, and county-specific legal frameworks that are often overlooked but critically important for citizens, businesses, and local governance.

This project represents a significant effort to digitize, organize, and provide intuitive access to American legal literature spanning every jurisdiction, even the small ones.

Features

  • - Comprehensive database of legal documents for 935 U.S. cities and counties
  • - Natural text search functionality
  • - Citation linking and cross-referencing
  • - Text summarization of complex legal documents
  • - User-friendly interface for legal research

Data Sources

Our dataset compiles information from:

  • - Municipal ordinances
  • - County ordinances
  • - Legislative documents
  • - Administrative regulations
  • - Historical legal precedents

All documents are processed and standardized for consistent search and retrieval.
